# 5 Mouthwatering Pork Recipes to Warm Your Fall Evenings

As the leaves turn golden and the air grows crisp, there's nothing quite like the comforting aroma of pork simmering in the kitchen. At The Porky Pantry, we're all about celebrating the versatility and flavor of pork, and fall is the perfect time to dive into hearty, soul-warming dishes. Whether you're a seasoned home chef or just starting out, these five recipes will elevate your autumn meals. Let's get cooking!

## 1. Slow-Cooked Pulled Pork Sliders

Perfect for game days or casual gatherings, this tender pulled pork is a crowd-pleaser.

**Ingredients:**
- 4 lbs pork shoulder
- 1 cup barbecue sauce
- 1 onion, sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- Salt and pepper to taste

**Instructions:**
1. Season the pork with salt, pepper, and garlic. Place in a slow cooker with onions and barbecue sauce.
2. Cook on low for 8 hours until shreddable.
3. Shred the meat and serve on slider buns with coleslaw. 

Pro tip: Add a dash of apple cider vinegar for that tangy fall twist!

## 2. Apple Cider Pork Chops with Roasted Veggies

The sweet tartness of apples pairs beautifully with juicy pork chops.

**Ingredients:**
- 4 bone-in pork chops
- 2 cups apple cider
- 2 apples, sliced
- 1 lb Brussels sprouts
- Olive oil, salt, and thyme

**Instructions:**
1. Sear chops in a skillet, then deglaze with cider and add apples.
2. Simmer for 20 minutes until cooked through.
3. Roast veggies separately and serve alongside.

This dish screams autumn harvest—simple yet elegant.

## 3. Hearty Pork and Butternut Squash Stew

A one-pot wonder that's nutritious and filling.

**Ingredients:**
- 1.5 lbs pork stew meat
- 1 butternut squash, cubed
- 1 carrot, chopped
- 2 potatoes, diced
- Broth, sage, and bay leaves

**Instructions:**
1. Brown pork in a Dutch oven.
2. Add veggies, broth, and herbs; simmer for 1 hour.
3. Adjust seasoning and enjoy with crusty bread.

Ideal for chilly evenings when you want minimal cleanup.

## 4. Maple-Glazed Pork Tenderloin

Sweet, savory, and oh-so-tender.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 pork tenderloin (1.5 lbs)
- 1/4 cup maple syrup
- Dijon mustard
- Garlic and rosemary

**Instructions:**
1. Mix glaze ingredients and marinate pork for 30 minutes.
2. Roast at 375°F for 25-30 minutes, basting occasionally.
3. Rest and slice—pairs perfectly with fall greens.

This recipe highlights pork's natural sweetness with seasonal maple.

## 5. Pork Belly Ramen with Autumn Mushrooms

Elevate your soup game with this umami-packed bowl.

**Ingredients:**
- 1 lb pork belly, sliced
- Ramen noodles
- Shiitake mushrooms
- Soy sauce, ginger, and green onions
- Homemade broth

**Instructions:**
1. Crisp the pork belly in a pan.
2. Simmer broth with ginger and mushrooms.
3. Assemble bowls with noodles, pork, and toppings.

A fusion twist that's comforting and exotic.
